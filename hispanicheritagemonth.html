Hispanic Culture



Holidays

Día de los Reyes

Día de los Reyes, which is also known as the Three Kings' Day or Epiphany, is one of Nayeli and Janelle's favorite holidays. This day includes a feast with a special tradition of each guest choosing a biece of bread. Whoever chooses the bread piece hiding a plastic baby figurine will have to help host the next party.

Día de los Muertos

Día de los Muertos is Izel's favorite holiday. It is also known as the Day of the Dead, and it serves to celebrate the lives and achievements of of deceased relatives. This includes decorating tables with the relatives' photos, flowers, and objects that relate to their passions.

Día de Niños

Nayeli and Janelle shared their knowledge of Día de Niños with me. This day celebrates children and consists of parades, live bands, parties, and the cancellation of school. Children receive gifts and their favorite meals, alongside playing games such as Lotería This day also serves to bring awareness to less fortunate children, as gifts are donated to them as well. Each year, Janelle's grandmother gifts her and her sister a bracelet charm on Día de Niños.



Food

Pozole

Janelle's favorite food is her grandmother's pozole, which is a soup made of hominy, meat, shredded lettuce, cabbage, chile peppers, onion, garlic, radishes, avocado, and salsa.

Mole

Mole is a type of sauce that is often served with chicken, enchilada, tacos, and burritos. Nayeli's favorite mole dish is spicy pork with rice. Izel cooks her mole differently, as she adds Abuelita Hot Chocolate powder to make it sweet.

Flan

Flan is a dessert consisting of eggs, sweetened condensed milk, cream, and whole milk. It is a caramel pudding/custard with caramel sauce. Chocoflan has chocolate cake benath the flan.

Birria

One of Janelle's favorites is Birria, a soup made of goat meat abodo, garlic, cumin, bay leaves, and thyme. The soup is slow-stewed at a low heat.

Arroz con Leche

Arroz con leche, also known as rice pudding, is made of rice soaked in water or milk. Cinnamon, vanilla, and raisins are often added for flavor.

Horchata

Nayeli enjoys Horchata, which is a sweet rice milk drink consisting of rice, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon.

Agua de Jamaica

Agua de Jamaica is a hibiscus iced tea made from dried hibiscus flowers, water, and sugar.

Pasteries

Some of Janelle's favorite pasteries are Conchas and Pan de pan. Conchas are round, striped sweet bread rolls, and Pan de Pan (Budin De Pan) is a bread pudding dish.



Traditions

Remedies for Illness

Nayeli and Janelle shared that when ill, they are told to drink Sprite and put Vaporub under their noses/on their chests. For sore throats, they drink honey with lemon, and to counteract stomach aches, they take picot (anatcid packets).

Gathering Ettiquete

At parties, guests are expected to greet everyone when arriving and to say goodbye when leaving. If guests do not properly greet others, they are considered rude and uneducated.

Blankets

We share the tradition of passing down heavy, weighted blankets. We often refer to these as "coma blankets" due to their extremely thick fleece and overpowering warmth. The blankets often feature majestic animals, especially tigers and horses.
